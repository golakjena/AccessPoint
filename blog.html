Borrowing from a Specialty Hospitality Lender Versus a Community Bank: Pros and Cons

Now that the lending environment in the hotel industry is loosening up, you may be looking for a source to finance your next endeavor. Borrowers today are typically able to get more competitive financing deals due to the increased competition among lenders. However, there is no “one stop shop” when it comes to lending. Even though you may have an existing business relationship with your local bank, they may not be the right fit for a short-term bridge loan. Or perhaps you met with a specialty lender at a recent hotel industry conference that you would like to pursue a new construction loan with however, they might not be the right fit for that type of loan. Local, community banks and hospitality lenders each have their roles in the hotel financing space, and finding the right partner for your project is key.

Community bank

Hotel owners often look to their local community bank for financing, especially if there is already a pre-established personal or business relationship, since community banks offer business banking services, as well as lending services. Borrowers can typically best use these services for new hotel construction or longer-term financing projects. When considering a loan with a community bank, it is important to remember that these institutions may be restricted to only their local community therefore, if your project is outside of the geographic market areas served by the institution, they would not be a viable partner. Another restriction to keep in mind is often only a certain percentage of community banks’ loan portfolio can be invested in the hotel industry in an effort to evenly distribute risk across all types of industries. Additionally, community banks are heavily regulated, which can make for a lengthier underwriting process, at times up to 60 days to fund a loan.

Hospitality specialty lenders ideal for CapEx and bridge loans

Hotel franchisees are very often looking for short term capital, such as a CapEx loan, to support a renovation, brand refresh or brand conversion. For these common financing needs, it is most appropriate to look to a specialty hotel lender. A lender focused exclusively on the hotel industry knows the nuances associated with hotel properties and hotel projects. A hospitality lender is also open to offering short-term loans for hotel acquisitions, or to refinance an investment. Specialty lenders are not as heavily regulated as traditional banks therefore, they can close a loan much more quickly than a community bank. In some cases, this means you could have the necessary funds for a CapEx loan project in as little as two days and a bridge loan funded in about two weeks. This allows you to stabilize, renovate and obtain long-term financing for your hotel property, which saves money in the long run.

No one lender can "do it all"

With market activity in the hotel industry trending up, it is important to know what type of lender can supply a loan that meets the necessities of both your project and your timeframe. Most lenders serve a niche and offer a specific product whether it’s a CMBS loan, SBA loan, new construction loan, CapEx or bridge loan. With many choices, hotel owners must be able to evaluate the pros and cons of borrowing from a community bank vs. a specialty hotel lender and which one will offer the most appropriate loan product for your project. This decision will not only fund your current project but will also support your overall profit objectives.
